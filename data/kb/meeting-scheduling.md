# Meeting Request & Scheduling Guide

## How to Request a Meeting

### Before Sending Meeting Request

**Prepare Your Request:**

1. Determine the purpose and expected duration
2. Identify all required attendees
3. Check meeting organizer's calendar for available time slots
4. Gather any necessary supporting materials or agenda
5. Determine if meeting should be virtual, in-person, or hybrid

**Meeting Duration Guidelines:**

- Status updates: 15-30 minutes
- One-on-one check-ins: 30 minutes
- Team meetings: 45-60 minutes
- Project kick-off or planning: 60-90 minutes
- Customer presentations: 60-120 minutes
- Training or onboarding: 2-4 hours

### Requesting a Meeting via Email

**Include in Your Request:**

1. **Clear Subject**: "Meeting Request: [Topic] - [Date] [Time]"
2. **Purpose**: Briefly state why you need the meeting
3. **Duration**: Specify how long you need (e.g., "30 minutes")
4. **Proposed Time**: Offer 3 time slot options with time zone
5. **Meeting Type**: Specify Virtual (Zoom/Teams), In-Person (Conference Room), or Hybrid
6. **Agenda**: For scheduled meetings, provide bullet-point agenda
7. **Required Materials**: Note if attendees need to review anything beforehand

**Example:**

```
Subject: Meeting Request: Q4 Budget Review - Nov 15, 2-3 PM PT

Hi Sarah,

I'd like to schedule a 1-hour meeting to review Q4 budget projections
and discuss spending for the new initiative.

Available times:
- Tuesday, Nov 15: 2-3 PM PT
- Wednesday, Nov 16: 10-11 AM PT
- Thursday, Nov 17: 3-4 PM PT

Would any of these times work? This will be a Zoom virtual meeting.

Please review the attached budget summary beforehand.

Thanks,
John
```

## Calendar Management Best Practices

### Sending Calendar Invitations

**When creating a calendar invite:**

1. Include the same level of detail as you would in an email request
2. Set the meeting location or video conference link clearly
3. Add agenda in the meeting description
4. Attach relevant documents
5. Set reminder: 15 minutes before virtual, 30 minutes before in-person
6. Specify if attendance is required or optional

**Response Expectations:**

- Always respond (Accept/Decline/Tentative) within 24 hours
- If you need to decline, suggest alternative time or attendee
- For tentative responses, follow up within 48 hours with confirmation

### Calendar Blocking

**Protect Your Productivity:**

- Block 2-3 hours daily for focused work
- Block lunch time (12 PM - 1 PM) unless expecting meetings
- Block time for important projects or deadlines
- Mark as "Busy" for blocks you can't move
- It's acceptable to block 100% of available meeting time if unrealistic

### Managing your Calendar

**Best Practices:**

- Check calendar before committing to meetings in emails
- Avoid back-to-back meetings - schedule 15-minute buffers minimum
- Include travel time for in-person meetings (10-15 minutes)
- Block time on day before major meetings for preparation
- Update team monthly calendar - indicate vacation, conferences, focused work periods

## Virtual Meeting Guidelines

### Before the Meeting

**Preparation:**

1. Test audio and video 5 minutes early
2. Ensure stable internet connection (wired preferred)
3. Minimize background distractions
4. Use professional background or virtual blur
5. Have necessary documents within reach
6. Close unrelated browser tabs and applications
7. Silence phone notifications

### During the Meeting

**Professional Conduct:**

- Start meetings on time
- Introduce yourself if meeting new participants
- Mute microphone when not speaking (especially in large meetings)
- Make eye contact with camera when speaking
- Avoid multitasking - don't check email or messages
- Take notes on agenda items
- Participate actively

### After the Meeting

**Follow-up:**

- Send meeting notes or action items within 2 hours
- Assign ownership for any agreed action items
- Include deadlines for deliverables
- Schedule follow-up meeting if needed
- Thank attendees, especially if requesting significant time

## In-Person Meeting Logistics

### Conference Room Booking

Rooms available:

- **Small (4-6 people)**: Room A, B, C - no reservation needed, first-come basis
- **Medium (8-12 people)**: Conference Room 1, 2 - reserve via calendar
- **Large (20+ people)**: Board Room - requires 3 days advance notice, director approval

**Amenities:**

- All rooms: Table, chairs, whiteboard, video conferencing system
- Board Room: Large AV display, wireless presentation
- Kitchen: Complimentary coffee, tea, snacks

### Meeting Room Setup

**Arrive 10 minutes early to:**

1. Test video conferencing system for hybrid attendees
2. Connect laptop to display
3. Write agenda on whiteboard
4. Ensure temperature is comfortable
5. Verify water and refreshments

## Declining Meeting Requests

**When You Can't Attend:**

1. **Decline promptly** - within 24 hours of request
2. **Provide reason** (optional) - "Have conflicting project deadline"
3. **Suggest alternative** - "Can you reschedule to Thursday?"
4. **Recommend substitute** - "John has similar expertise and can attend"
5. **Offer alternative** - "Happy to review notes and discuss afterward"

**Always decline if:**

- You lack required context or expertise
- The meeting time conflicts with critical deadline
- The meeting lacks clear purpose or agenda
- Attendance isn't critical to decision

Declining respectfully preserves relationships and improves meeting efficiency.

## Special Circumstances

### Timezone Coordination

For international meetings:

1. Use converter to verify time in everyone's timezone
2. Rotate meeting times to share inconvenience equally
3. Record meetings for those who can't attend live
4. Provide transcript for asynchronous review

### Large Group Meetings (15+ people)

- Limit meeting to 50 minutes maximum
- Use structured agenda with time allocations
- Designate facilitator to manage discussion
- Provide written summary afterward
- Consider using meeting platform features (breakout rooms, polls)

### Emergency/Urgent Meetings

For issues requiring immediate attention:

1. Use "URGENT" in subject line
2. Specify time window, not specific time slot
3. Note reason for urgency
4. Be prepared to meet within 30 minutes of request
5. Use instant messaging for critical alerts
