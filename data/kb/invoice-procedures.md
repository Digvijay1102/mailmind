# Invoice Processing Procedures

## Standard Invoice Workflow

### 1. Receipt & Initial Review

All invoices received through the customer email system go through automatic classification. Invoices are categorized by urgency based on:

- Due date proximity (overdue = high urgency)
- Amount (large invoices assigned higher priority)
- Vendor relationship (critical vendors flagged)

### 2. Approval Process

- **Invoices under $5,000**: Approved by department manager if all details match PO
- **Invoices $5,000 - $50,000**: Requires both department manager and finance director approval
- **Invoices over $50,000**: Requires CFO approval plus legal review if not standard vendor

### 3. Payment Processing

Once approved, invoices are processed within 5 business days. Standard payment terms are NET 30. Early payment discounts (2% for NET 10) are applied when cash flow permits.

## Invoice Content Requirements

All invoices must include:

- Clear invoice number and date
- Vendor name and tax ID (if applicable)
- Our purchase order number (when applicable)
- Itemized breakdown of goods/services
- Total amount with tax clearly separated
- Payment terms and due date
- Vendor payment instructions (bank details or address)

## Special Cases

### Recurring Invoices

For vendors with standing orders (e.g., monthly subscriptions, SaaS licenses), set up recurring approval rules. These bypass normal review if within $2,000 monthly limit and within 10% of expected amount.

### Disputed Invoices

If invoice amount or details don't match our purchase order:

1. Forward to vendor for clarification
2. Hold payment pending resolution
3. Flag in system as "under dispute"
4. Communicate with department manager and vendor liaison

### Partial Invoices

If receiving multiple shipments against single PO, vendor may issue partial invoices. These are clearly marked with milestone reference and expected final invoice date. Process each partial invoice when goods are received and verified.

## Tax Compliance

### US Invoices

- Verify vendor has W-9 on file (required for payments)
- Ensure sales tax applied correctly for taxable goods

### International Invoices

- VAT/GST must be segregated from gross amount
- Verify vendor registration number
- Convert to USD using date-of-invoice rate

### 1099 Reporting

Track all vendor payments. Vendors receiving >$600 annually must have W-9 on file for 1099 reporting requirement.
