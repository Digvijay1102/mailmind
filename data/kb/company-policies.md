# Company Policies & Guidelines

## Communication Standards

### Email Response Times

- Urgent issues (marked as urgent_alert): Response within 1 hour
- High priority (urgency >= 7): Response within 4 hours
- Standard requests (urgency 4-6): Response within 24 hours
- Low priority (urgency < 4): Response within 48 hours

### Professional Email Etiquette

- Use professional greeting and closing
- Keep emails concise and focused
- Use clear subject lines that summarize the email content
- Avoid using ALL CAPS (considered shouting)
- Include relevant context and previous communication thread when necessary

## Data Protection & Privacy

### Confidential Information

Never share customer personal information (PII), financial data, or proprietary information via email. Use secure portal for sensitive documents. If confidential data is mistakenly sent, notify security@company.com immediately.

### External Communication

When communicating with external parties, only share information explicitly authorized by your manager. Do not discuss company strategy, pricing, or internal processes with customers unless required.

### Password & Credentials

Never share passwords over email. Use secure password manager for credential sharing. Report compromised credentials to IT immediately.

## Meeting & Calendar Management

### Scheduling Practices

- Check attendee calendars before sending meeting invites
- Provide clear agenda for meetings
- Schedule at least 3 days in advance for regular meetings, 1 day minimum for urgent meetings
- Confirm attendance 24 hours before meeting time

### Virtual Meeting Etiquette

- Test audio/video setup 5 minutes before meeting
- Use professional background or blur personal space
- Minimize distractions and side conversations
- Start and end meetings on time

### Calendar Blocking

Block time for focused work and communication reviews. Avoid back-to-back meetings when possible.

## Vendor Management

### Preferred Vendors

We maintain relationships with approved vendors to ensure quality and cost efficiency. New vendors require manager approval and may require evaluation period.

### Procurement Process

1. Submit request to your manager with business justification
2. Obtain three quotes for purchases over $10,000
3. Verify vendor is not on exclusion list
4. Process through appropriate approval workflow
5. Maintain documentation for audit purposes

### Performance Evaluation

Vendors are evaluated quarterly on:

- Quality of deliverables
- On-time delivery rate
- Responsiveness to issues
- Pricing competitiveness

## Account Access & Delegation

### Access Levels

- **Standard User**: View and respond to assigned emails
- **Manager**: Approve invoices and incoming requests, manage team emails
- **Admin**: System configuration, user management, full audit access

### Out of Office

When taking extended leave:

1. Set up out-of-office message with alternative contact
2. Delegate email handling to designated team member
3. Notify relevant customers and vendors
4. Review important emails upon return

### Request Escalation

If uncertain about how to handle a request, escalate to your immediate manager rather than delaying response. Include context and reason for escalation.

## Performance Expectations

### Service Level Agreements (SLAs)

- First response to customer inquiry: 4 hours during business hours
- Issue resolution: Based on priority level (same day for critical, 5 days for standard)
- Email accuracy: 99% correct first response rate expected
- Knowledge base usage: Leverage KB for 80%+ of support responses

### Quality Standards

- All responses reviewed for accuracy, tone, and completeness
- Follow established response templates where applicable
- Maintain professional tone in all communications
- Document resolutions for knowledge base improvement
